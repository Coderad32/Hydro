Documentation

Sigil Matrix is a visual reference for symbolic combinations, often used in programming, cryptography, and creative design.

  • Hover over any symbol to see its meaning or usage.
  • Green-highlighted sigils indicate special "AUTH" or reserved combinations.
  • The matrix is responsive and works on mobile devices.
  • For more information, visit the linked resources above.

To contribute or suggest improvements, please contact the project maintainer.

Sigil Tokens Reference

!

Exclamation: Often used for logical NOT, negation, or to indicate importance.
Examples: !true === false, !important in CSS.

@

At: Used for decorators, email addresses, or referencing users.
Examples: @username in social media, @decorator in Python.

#

Hash: Used for comments, identifiers, or hashtags.
Examples: #include in C/C++, #hashtag on Twitter.

$

Dollar: Used for variables, string interpolation, or currency.
Examples: $variable in PHP, ${expression} in JS templates.

%

Percent: Used for modulo operations or percentages.
Examples: n % 2 for even/odd, width: 50%; in CSS.

^

Caret: Used for bitwise XOR, exponentiation, or as a control character.
Examples: a ^ b in many languages, 2^3 for powers.

&

Ampersand: Used for logical AND, references, or concatenation.
Examples: a & b (bitwise AND), &entity; in HTML.

*

Asterisk: Used for multiplication, pointers, or wildcards.
Examples: a * b, *args in Python, *.txt for file patterns.

( )

Parentheses: Used for grouping, function calls, or order of operations.
Examples: f(x), (a + b) * c.
